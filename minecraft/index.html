Homework #5: Custom Visualization Design

Nithin Kumar Surineni • nsurine1@asu.edu

About this Exhibit — Design, Method & Category

Goal. Help museum visitors decide “Which Minecraft food is best?” by showing how each item contributes to hearts (health proxy), hunger, and how many items reach full 20 hearts.

Dataset. Food.csv (Minecraft foods). We use attributes name, type, hunger,, and (if present) hearts/heal. If hearts are missing, we visualize a clear exhibit proxy: hearts ≈ hunger × 0.5.

Marks & Channels.

  • Heart glyph row — mark: PNG heart/drumstick icons; channels: count (full/half/empty), order (left→right), opacity (overlay for state).
  • Bars — mark: length bars; channels: length for hunger magnitudes.
  • Badge — text marks for name/type + computed items to full.
  • Avatar window — contextual anchor (no data encoding) that increases engagement.

Interactions. Left/right arrows & keyboard to browse foods; Sort toolbar to reorder by Hearts or Hunger; metric chips update live.

Custom Visualization Category. Combinatorial — a non-standard composition combining: heart/drumstick icon rows (custom glyphs), quantitative bars, avatar cut-out scene, and narrative controls in one graphic.

Why it’s custom. This is not a stock chart; it fuses multiple encodings into a themed, single-canvas museum layout with tailored iconography and a masked avatar window.

Notes. The exhibit focuses on ≥30 items (full CSV). If you place PNGs in foods/ (slug of item name), the item art appears automatically.
